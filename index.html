With no title, no image information
and with the ISBN number as the
publisher’s name, this artist’s book creates an anonymous impression, which is enhanced by the understated design and the use of standard
paper and a standard printing process. The anonymity suits the serial nature
of the digitally drawn images by
Swiss artist Tobias Madison, as well
as their essential reproducibility.
As the images were printed from
digital data, they can never really be genuine originals, but neither are they mere copies – even in book form.

Virginia Woolf – an exhibition inspired by her writings is a catalog produced for the eponymous exhibition in TateSt Ives (February 10 – April 29, 2018).

The graphic concept is based on the exhibition architecture : the catalog
is symmetrically structured, beginning with liberally full-bleed landscapes.
The deeper the viewer delves into
the physical exhibition, or the deeper the reader delves into the core of this book, the more intimate the subjects and works become. The print space reacts accordingly, contracting towards the middle of the book, as though
the contents sought protection in the
gutter margin. The offset white grounding used as an additional color for the catalog printing creates a spatial effect behind the isolated sculptural objects. The works presented in this book are surrounded in a similar manner by white traces of the drawings by France-Lise McGurn that were executed directly on the walls and around the works displayed in the exhibition.
